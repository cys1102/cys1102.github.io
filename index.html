Electric Vehicle Analysis¶

Yunsung Chung¶

Introduction¶

Electric vehicles (EVs) have seen a significant rise in popularity over the past decade. Their growth can be attributed to advancements in battery technology, increasing environmental awareness, and supportive government policies. As the transportation landscape evolves, understanding the trends and factors influencing the growth of EVs becomes crucial for stakeholders ranging from policymakers to automobile manufacturers.

This notebook aims to delve deep into the electric vehicles using the primary dataset:

Electric Vehicle Population Data (State of Washington): This dataset provides detailed information on electric vehicles registered through the State of Washington, including attributes such as the vehicle make, model, electric range, and geographic details. The dataset is provided by the State of Washington and publicly available at link.

Vehicle Fuel Type Count by ZIP code (State of California): This dataset provides vehicle counts by ZIP code in State of California including model year, make, fuel type, etc. This dataset can be downloaded at link.

Electric Vehicle Charging Station Locations: This dataset provides locations of electric charging stations in the United States. You can download the dataset at link.

Personal Income by County: This dataset provides the average income by county. It's publictly available at link

US Zipcodes to County State to FIPS Crosswalk: This data includes ZIP Code, County Name, State, FIPS, etc. This data is publictly available at link

Questions to Address:¶

  1. Can we predict EV sales based on infrastructure and income?
  2. Do charging stations infrastructure & income influence EV adoption rates?
  3. Considering where you live and income, can we devise a personalized recommendation on whether one should purchase an EV?

Dataset are suitable?¶

  • Elctric Vehicle Population Data: Ideal for understanding the popularity of EV models and makes, the distribution of EVs by type across regions, and the electic range of vehicles by model and make. It provides detailed attributes for each registered electric vehicle, such as the vehicle make, model, type, and electric range, which are essential for this analysis.
  • Vehicle Fuel Type Count by ZIP code: This dataset provides a geographic dimension to vehicle distribution, which is crucial for correlating infrastructure with vehicle sales and adoption patterns.
  • Electric Vehicle Charging Station Locations: Critical for assessing the alignment of EV infrastructure with the distribution of electric vehicles, this dataset underpins analyses of current infrastructure adequacy.
  • US Zipcodes to County State to FIPS Crosswalk: This can be used for converting zipcodes to FIPS and vice versa, which makes possible to analyze our data per county.

Goal:¶

By the end of this analysis, we aim to gain a comprehensive understanding of the current electric vehicle landscape. Leveraging the insights drawn from the datasets, we will attempt to predict the future purchase rate of electric vehicles per county. In addition, we plan to build a personalized recommendation model whether you should purchase EVs or not based on local charging station infrastructure and personal income.

Model¶

1. Linear Regression Model for Predicting EV Sales¶

For predicting EV sales, a linear regression model will be utilized. The primary predictor for this model is the number of charging stations based on the assumption that charging infrastructure availability significantly influences consumer decisions to purchase EVs. The model will also incorporate average income per county. As these factors could show linear relationships with EV sales, linear regression is chosen for its effectiveness in capturing linear correlations among these features.

2. K-Means Clustering for EV Purchase Recommendation¶

The recommendation model is designed to assist in making personalized EV purchase decisions. It will consider factors like income and the proximity of charging stations to an individual's home or workplace. Instead of a classification approach, this model will implement K-means clustering to create two distinct clusters: 'likely to buy' and 'unlikely to buy' an EV. This simplification aims to provide clear, actionable insights for potential EV buyers, categorizing them based on their likelihood to benefit from an EV purchase.
