Introduction¶

Electric vehicles (EVs) have seen a significant rise in popularity over the past decade. Their growth can be attributed to advancements in battery technology, increasing environmental awareness, and supportive government policies. As the transportation landscape evolves, understanding the trends and factors influencing the growth of EVs becomes crucial for stakeholders ranging from policymakers to automobile manufacturers.

This notebook aims to delve deep into the electric vehicles using the primary dataset:

Electric Vehicle Population Data: This dataset provides detailed information on electric vehicles registered in various locations, including attributes such as the vehicle make, model, typewe, and geographic details. The dataset is provided by the State of Washington and publicly available at link.

Questions to Address:¶

  1. Which electric vehicle models and makes are the most popular?
  2. How are different types of electric vehicles distributed across various regions?
  3. How does the electric range of vehicles vary across different models and makes?
  4. Can we predict the future sales of electric vehicles based on data?
  5. What attributes does effect the final sales?

Goal:¶

By the end of this analysis, we aim to gain a comprehensive understanding of the current electric vehicle landscape. Leveraging the insights drawn from the datasets, we will attempt to predict the future purchase rate of electric vehicles, shedding light on the potential trajectory of the EV market in the coming years.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)¶

In the EDA section, we'll visually explore the Electric Vehicle Population Data to identify patterns, relationships, and insights that can guide our further analysis.

2.1 Vehicle Count by Year
The starting point is to understand the growth trend of electric vehicles over the years. By plotting the count of vehicles by their model year, we can recognize the evolution of electric vehicle popularity.

In [18]:
# Vehicle Count by Year
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
sns.countplot(data=ev_data, x='Model Year', hue='Model Year', order=sorted(ev_data['Model Year'].unique()), palette="viridis")
plt.title('Vehicle Count by Year')
plt.ylabel('Number of Vehicles')
plt.xlabel('Model Year')
plt.xticks(rotation=45)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
